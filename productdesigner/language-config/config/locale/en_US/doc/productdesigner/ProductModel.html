Product Designer Home Page

A PolicyCenter product defines one of a carrier’s product offerings. A product offering is defined by a product model pattern and includes:

• Product – Container for one or more policy lines, question sets, and audit schedules. Also referred to as a line of business.

• Policy lines – Types of insurance provided within a product. A product can contain one policy line or multiple policy lines. A multi-line policy is a package policy. Each policy line defines coverables and the coverages protect them, together with conditions, exclusions, and coverage terms.

• Question sets – Collections of questions presented within PolicyCenter that gather information about an applicant. Answers to questions can be used to evaluate risk, determine offering availability, and gather general information.

• Audit schedules – Specifications that define the timing of audits and premium reports for a product. Audits are optional activities of a product offering, but when used are defined by an audit schedule.

• System tables – Lists of metadata that are needed to support the business logic of a product.

PolicyCenter stores product definitions as patterns. PolicyCenter uses these patterns during the submission process to generate instances of policies or the subcomponents of policies.

Working with the Product Model

Use the Navigation panel on the left to locate and select any node within the product model.

 

 

Node with hidden descendants. Click to expand the node and reveal its descendants. Click the node’s label to open its corresponding page.

 

Expanded node. Click to collapse the node and hide its descendants. Click the node’s label to open its corresponding page.

 

Node with no descendants. Click the node or its label to open the corresponding page.

Filtering the Tree

Use the text box at the top of the Navigation panel to filter the tree. After typing one or more characters in the filter box, the filter hides every node except nodes whose names or codes begin with characters you typed. The code is the portion at the end of the label enclosed in square brackets. Note that the filtered tree also shows the parent nodes of each matching item, to help identify its location in the product model.

Click the adjacent X to clear the filter and restore all nodes to the tree.
